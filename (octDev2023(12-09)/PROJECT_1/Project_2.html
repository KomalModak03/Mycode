A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a Missile Man of India. He is popularly known as the People’s President of India. His full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam. He was a great scientist and 11th President of India. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in a poor Tamil Muslim family on 15th of October in 1931 to the Jainulabudeen and Ashiamma. Kalam started supporting his family financially in his early age. He has completed his graduation from the St Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli in 1954 and Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, Chennai in 1960. Kalam joined the DRDO (Defense Research and Development Organization) as a scientist where he designed a small helicopter for the Indian Army. He also worked under Dr. Vikram Sarabhai as part of INCOSPAR committee. Later, he joined the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) in 1969 as project director of India’s first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). Because of his great contributions for the development of ballistic missiles in India, he will be forever known as the “Missile Man of India”. The successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests of 1998 have also involved his important role.

Biography

  • Renowned Scientist: Abdul Kalam, born 1931, was a distinguished Indian aerospace engineer pivotal in space and missile programs.
  • "People's President": Serving as India's 11th President (2002-2007), Kalam was celebrated for his accessible and humble demeanor.
  • Author of "Wings of Fire": His autobiography, "Wings of Fire," chronicles his journey from humble beginnings to scientific eminence.
  • Educational Visionary: Post-presidency, Kalam focused on education and youth, inspiring millions with his vision for a knowledge-driven India.
  • Legacy of Inspiration: Despite his 2015 passing, Kalam's legacy endures, influencing generations with his commitment to science, education, and national development.
